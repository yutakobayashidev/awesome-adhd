# What makes work sustainable for adults with ADHD?

Source extracted from PubMed Central on 2026-07-23.

## Bibliographic facts

- Article: What makes work sustainable for adults with ADHD? Interpreting workplace strain and support through Reddit data.
- Venue/source: Frontiers / PMC page extracted 2026-07-23.
- Method: reflexive thematic analysis of 277 workplace-related r/ADHD discussion threads from 2023-2025, containing 39,750 comments and 1,226,113 words.

## Key captured findings

- Workplace features such as meetings, deadlines, disclosure, structure, and flexibility were not inherently demands or resources. Their effect depended on context, reliability, clarity, autonomy, and whether they fit the worker's regulation needs.
- Participants described a mismatch between ADHD work rhythms and conventional productivity models: nonlinear productivity could be constrained by fixed hours, visibility norms, and expectations of constant output.
- Flexibility helped when it allowed self-regulation of attention, energy, environment, and work rhythm; the same flexibility could hurt when it removed structure and accountability.
- Meetings created hidden executive-function labor: rapid auditory processing, working memory, note-taking, later recall, time tracking, and prospective memory.
- Written documentation, clear expectations, reminders, and collaborative structure reduced the need to hold everything internally.
- The paper frames ADHD strain as arising where workplace structures are misaligned with individual regulation needs, not as an inherent inability to work.

## Relevance

This is a strong source for “文脈不協”: the same tool or practice can support or harm depending on implementation. The design object is not meeting-vs-async in the abstract, but fit between communication mode, regulation needs, and accountability structure.
