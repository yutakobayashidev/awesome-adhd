# Toward Neurodivergent-Aware Productivity: A Systems and AI-Based Human-in-the-Loop Framework for ADHD-Affected Professionals

Source extracted from arXiv HTML on 2026-07-23.

## Bibliographic facts

- arXiv: 2507.06864.
- Topic: conceptual framework for a privacy-first, AI/ML, human-in-the-loop productivity assistant for ADHD-affected professionals.
- Evidence base includes a preliminary survey of about 25 self/professionally diagnosed ADHD-affected digital workers. The paper is conceptual and early-stage; no deployment outcome data.

## Key captured findings

- Participants reported task prioritization difficulty, context switching fatigue, and “attention crash” moments during unstructured work periods and transitions between tasks or meetings.
- Participants wanted quiet, customizable, behavior-rhythm-responsive interventions rather than gamified motivation, hard deadlines, or intrusive reminders.
- Proposed architecture: on-device behavior sensing, voice interface, adaptive feedback engine, “Where Was I?” recall layer, soft nudges, and digital body doubling.
- Privacy is central: behavioral cues such as tab switching, app focus, and inactivity are processed locally; the paper argues that workplace exposure of attention drift or time blindness could deepen stigma and masking.
- AI is framed as co-regulator, not taskmaster: soft invitations, ambient presence, user override, and human-in-the-loop calibration.

## Relevance

This is directly useful for Toymaker/OpenBrief AI support: AI should help reconstruct context, lower transition cost, and provide consentful co-regulation without turning attention data into workplace surveillance.
