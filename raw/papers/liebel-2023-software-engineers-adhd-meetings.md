# Challenges, Strengths, and Strategies of Software Engineers with ADHD: A Case Study

Source extracted from arXiv HTML on 2026-07-23.

## Bibliographic facts

- Authors: Grischa Liebel, Nicolas Langlois, Kiev Gama.
- arXiv: 2312.05029.
- Method: exploratory qualitative case study; interviews with 19 software engineers with ADHD and discussion with 4 software-engineering managers.

## Key captured findings

- ADHD software engineers reported challenges with task organization, estimation, attention, relation to others, physical and mental health.
- Context switching was difficult. One participant described needing nearly an hour to load the structure of a task into mind, only to be interrupted by a casual question.
- Participating in meetings was difficult for 9 interviewees. They reported being easily distracted, unable to follow tangents, and finding unprepared meetings wasteful.
- Communication style could be misread as rude or confusing; one participant said thoughts were easier to express in code than in words.
- Useful strategies included body doubling, clear communication, adapting meeting formats, shorter/working meetings, agendas, fidget tools, removing distracting devices, written clarity, and flexible work modes.

## Design note

The paper supports a shift from “more meetings” to “higher-context, lower-surprise collaboration”: agenda before, written trace after, clear owner/next action, and optional body-doubling or working sessions only where synchronous presence is doing real scaffolding.
