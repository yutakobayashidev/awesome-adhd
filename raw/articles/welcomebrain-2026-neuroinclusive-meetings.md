# Rethinking Meetings: How to Run Neuroinclusive Meetings That Work for Everyone

Source extracted on 2026-07-23.

## Source type

Practice-oriented neuroinclusive meeting guide. This is not a peer-reviewed research paper, but it provides concrete design patterns consistent with the research findings.

## Key captured points

- Traditional meetings can exclude neurodivergent employees through rapid-fire discussion, sensory load, implicit social norms, and executive-function demands.
- Before scheduling, ask whether email, shared documents, project tools, or asynchronous collaboration would work better.
- Reducing unnecessary meetings especially helps people who find constant context switching difficult.
- Send agendas at least 24 hours in advance so participants can prepare thoughts and expectations.
- Provide multiple contribution modes: live chat, shared documents before/during/after meetings, audio/video responses outside real-time discussion.
- Do not treat silence as lack of engagement; some people need extra processing time.
- Alternative formats include brainwriting, turn-based discussion, and asynchronous meetings using Loom, Slack, or Miro.
- Follow-ups should include clear next steps, owners, and meeting notes.

## Relevance

Useful as a checklist for turning research into product defaults: meeting necessity check, agenda before, multiple channels during, written trace after.
