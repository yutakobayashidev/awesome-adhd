# Focusmate homepage

Source: https://www.focusmate.com
Captured: 2026-07-23
Source type: official product homepage

## Key source excerpts

Focusmate describes itself as “Virtual coworking for getting anything done” and “Focused. Productive. Together.” It offers video-based coworking sessions with another person present while each participant works on their own task.

The homepage presents common task examples including watering plants, reviewing a research paper, booking a flight, responding to email, cleaning a room, preparing a lesson, practicing guitar, doing code review, making dinner, studying for an exam, planning a day, writing, meditating, exercising, bedtime routines, and journaling.

Focusmate emphasizes flexible 25-, 50-, and 75-minute sessions. It says users can book a session, get matched with another community member, join video calls directly from the browser on desktop or mobile, sync sessions with a calendar, and check in with the partner at the end to celebrate progress.

The homepage says Focusmate has 12 million+ completed sessions, 500 million+ minutes of focus, and users in 150+ countries.

Focusmate's FAQ says it is for anyone who wants to get things done. It says users can do most kinds of work as long as they stay on camera: desk work, cleaning, cooking, art, music, writing, reading, or at-home exercise. It defines body doubling as working on any task with another person present, without them participating in the task. The page states that another person’s presence, in real life or virtual, can help users stay on track and get things done, and can boost motivation.

The FAQ says Focusmate pairs users with a community member somewhere in the world, and users can favorite partners they enjoyed working with. It says no extra video app is required because sessions are booked and joined on Focusmate, and calendar invites are sent. It says Focusmate is free for up to three sessions per week and Focusmate Plus offers unlimited sessions.

## ADHD-wiki interpretation notes

This source is an official product page, not a clinical trial or independent efficacy review. It should be treated as a product description and a concrete implementation of virtual body doubling/accountability, not as evidence that Focusmate treats ADHD symptoms.

The main ADHD-relevant design pattern is moving task initiation and sustained attention from private willpower into a scheduled social container: a booked time, camera presence, a visible partner, an initial commitment, and an end-of-session check-in.

Privacy and safety caveat: because Focusmate uses live video coworking and calendar-like session scheduling, users may expose face, room, household context, work materials, task names, schedule patterns, and other sensitive information. It should be used with intentional camera framing, task disclosure limits, and awareness of workplace/school confidentiality.
